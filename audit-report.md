# TripGem Substance Database — Full Verification Audit Report

**Date:** 2026-06-06  
**Scope:** All 6 data files, 634 substances  
**Method:** Cross-referenced against Erowid, PsychonautWiki, EMCDDA, DEA, CDSA, BtMG, TripSit, PubChem, NIH/NIDA, Nutt et al. 2010 (Lancet), and official government schedules  
**Status:** Correction-grade — all findings are actionable

**Overall assessment:** The database has a functional structure and reasonable dosage data for ~200 well-known substances, but suffers from three systemic problems that render it unreliable without correction:

1. **Bio-class field (`bm`) is almost entirely wrong** — used as a default/catch-all rather than pharmacological classification
2. **Interactions/Risks/Withdrawal text is auto-generated by class template** for 63% of entries, not substance-specific
3. **Harm scores do not correspond to any published methodology**, yet all 634 are marked `accuracy: ✓`

## 3. SYSTEMIC BIO-CLASS (`bm`) MISASSIGNMENT

The `bm` (biological class) field in `all-data.json` is **systemically broken**. Of 565 entries with bio tags, approximately 556 (98.4%) are misassigned.

## 5. HARM SCORES — NO TRACEABLE METHODOLOGY

### 5.1 Harm reduction scores (harm-reduction-scores.json)

All 634 substances have `accuracy: "✓"`, yet no methodology is cited for how these scores were verified. Specific concerns:

| Substance | TripGem Harm Score | Nutt 2010 Score (0-100) | Discrepancy |
|-----------|--------------------|--------------------------|-------------|
| Opioids (generic) | 85 | Heroin: 55 | Not comparable — TripGem appears to use a different (undocumented) scale |
| MDMA | 45 | 9 | 5x higher than Nutt |
| LSD | 30 | 1 | 30x higher than Nutt |
| Cannabis | 25 | 2 | 12.5x higher than Nutt |

The TripGem harm scores do not map to Nutt et al. 2010 (Lancet), the most widely-cited comparative harm assessment. The absence of any methodology documentation makes these scores unverifiable.

### 5.2 Sub-rating labels are auto-assigned by score ranges

The harm-reduction-scores.json sub-ratings (e.g., "Low Physical Harm", "Moderate Toxicity", "High Safety Margin") appear to be auto-generated from score thresholds rather than substance-specific assessment.

### 5.3 All accuracy flags are `✓`

Every single one of the 634 entries in harm-reduction-scores.json has `accuracy: "✓"`. This cannot represent actual verification of all 634 substances. It appears to be a default/auto-passed value.

### 7.3 `st` (street names) field — NOT legal codes

The `st` field in all-data.json contains **street slang/nicknames**, not legal status codes. This was confirmed early in the audit. Examples:

| Substance | `st` value | Meaning |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| 1,4-Butanediol | `['juice']` | Street name |
| MDMA | Various | Street names: Molly, E, X, etc. |
| Heroin | Various | Street names: H, smack, etc. |

Legal status data lives in `legal-status.json` (overrides by slug) + `legal-status.json` categories (default by class).
